Notes on Elements of Statistical Learning

26 November 2018 Machine Learning, Data Mining, Predictive Inference, Statistical Learning, Theory
Learning notes of ‘Elements of Statistical Learning’ written by Hastie, Tibshirani and Friedman. This book is a must-have if you want to lay a solid foundatation on machine learning from the frequentist point of view. I read the book and tried to re-write the methods in my own words to make sure that I fully understand them. The notes are not complete yet and it will be updated from time to time. See an electronic version of the book from the authors’ website https://web.stanford.edu/~hastie/ElemStatLearn/. (Thank the authors so much for the efforts to make the book available to us). If you are new to machine learning and you’d like to do some practice with hands-on code, another excellent book ‘An Introduction to Statistical Learning’ from http://www-bcf.usc.edu/~gareth/ISL/ could be read first. The supplemental videos at https://www.r-bloggers.com/in-depth-introduction-to-machine-learning-in-15-hours-of-expert-videos/ may also be helpful.

Notes on Statistical Methods for Reliability Data

25 November 2018 Reliability, Statistics, Quality
Learning notes on the first edition of ‘Statistical Methods for Reliability Data’ written by Meeker & Escobar. Please notes that the notes is NOT a substitution of the book as the notes are highly summarized and organized with my own way. This book covers a broad range of classical models and methods that are frequently used in industry, and the illustrations are very clear to follow for engineers and statisticians. It’s a must have for those who are interested in reliability, applied statistics and/or the practical models that are useful for industry. See the authors’ website http://www.public.iastate.edu/~stat533/meeker_escobar.html for more details on the contents of the book.
